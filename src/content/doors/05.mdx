<TLDR>
  <div className="space-y-4">
    <Quote minimal source="OWASP LLM05" href="https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm05-supply-chain-vulnerabilities/">
      The GenAI stack mixes hosted APIs, open-source weights, browser extensions, and plugins; compromise anywhere taints the entire agent runtime.
    </Quote>
    <List>
      <Item><b>Opaque dependencies.</b> Pre-built vector stores, embeddings, or guardrails may hide outdated libraries.</Item>
      <Item><b>Model artifacts.</b> Weights pulled from unofficial mirrors can be swapped for poisoned variants.</Item>
    </List>
  </div>
</TLDR>

<Section title="Where trust breaks" meta="CHAIN">
  <List>
    <Item><b>Model hosting.</b> API keys stored in CI/CD or notebooks leak to attackers who can exfiltrate data.</Item>
    <Item><b>Plugin registries.</b> Unsigned tools with broad permissions (filesystem, network) invite lateral movement.</Item>
    <Item><b>Data brokers.</b> Syndicated corpora may bundle malware-laced documents or licensing traps.</Item>
  </List>
</Section>

<Section title="Control plane" meta="ASSURANCE">
  <Steps>
    <Step n={1}><b>Inventory everything.</b> Maintain SBOM-style manifests for models, adapters, datasets, and plugins.</Step>
    <Step n={2}><b>Pin + verify.</b> Use checksums, signatures, and private registries before deploying artifacts.</Step>
    <Step n={3}><b>Monitor suppliers.</b> Subscribe to upstream advisories and rotate credentials when vendors disclose incidents.</Step>
  </Steps>

</Section>
