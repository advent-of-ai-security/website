<TLDR>
  <div className="space-y-4">
    <Quote minimal source="OWASP LLM08" href="https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm08-excessive-agency/">
      Granting an agent open-ended objectives, tool access, and persistence can let it modify systems far beyond the operator’s intent.
    </Quote>
    <List>
      <Item><b>Mission creep.</b> Agents tasked with vague goals improvise risky steps.</Item>
      <Item><b>Unbounded loops.</b> Autonomy without guardrails yields runaway chains of tool calls.</Item>
    </List>
  </div>
</TLDR>

<Section title="Risk accelerants" meta="AUTONOMY">
  <List>
    <Item><b>Long-lived memory.</b> Agents retain prior context and may pursue stale objectives.</Item>
    <Item><b>Privilege mismatch.</b> Agent tokens sometimes outrank the human requester.</Item>
    <Item><b>Tool sprawl.</b> The more connectors you bolt on, the wider the blast radius.</Item>
  </List>
</Section>

<Section title="Control patterns" meta="OPERATIONS">
  <Steps>
    <Step n={1}><b>Constrain scope.</b> Frame missions as concrete, single-shot tasks with measurable stopping conditions.</Step>
    <Step n={2}><b>Tiered permissions.</b> Map tools to sensitivity levels and require approvals for privileged tiers.</Step>
    <Step n={3}><b>Observability.</b> Stream plan/act logs to operators and provide “kill switch” controls.</Step>
  </Steps>
  <p className="text-[13px] text-black/70">Reference: <Link href="https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm08-excessive-agency/" target="_blank">OWASP LLM08</Link></p>
