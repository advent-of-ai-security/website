<TLDR>
  <div className="space-y-4">
    <Quote minimal source="OWASP LLM06" href="https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm06-sensitive-information-disclosure/">
      Without guardrails, LLMs can regurgitate secrets from training, reveal internal prompts, or dump stack traces that expose infrastructure.
    </Quote>
    <List>
      <Item><b>Prompt leaks.</b> Attackers coerce the model to reveal hidden system instructions.</Item>
      <Item><b>Data echoes.</b> Rare PII seen during training can resurface verbatim when queried cleverly.</Item>
    </List>
  </div>
</TLDR>

<Section title="Leak vectors" meta="EXFIL">
  <List>
    <Item><b>Memorized strings.</b> API keys, legal clauses, or proprietary code blocks repeated in datasets.</Item>
    <Item><b>Verbose errors.</b> Stack traces show internal hostnames, file paths, or data samples.</Item>
    <Item><b>Tool responses.</b> Agent-connected databases may answer unrestricted data pulls.</Item>
  </List>
</Section>

<Section title="Containment" meta="DATA" >
  <Steps>
    <Step n={1}><b>Data minimization.</b> Strip PII before training/fine-tuning; rely on synthetic or approved corpora.</Step>
    <Step n={2}><b>Prompt hardening.</b> Seal system prompts and decline to display them verbatim.</Step>
    <Step n={3}><b>Redaction layers.</b> Post-process outputs with pattern detectors for secrets, then mask or re-request.</Step>
  </Steps>
  <p className="text-[13px] text-black/70">Reference: <Link href="https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm06-sensitive-information-disclosure/" target="_blank">OWASP LLM06</Link></p>
</Section>
